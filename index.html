I Frighten the Competition

Effective enterprise software development is more than a methodology or - worse - collection of buzz words. It's the intersection of understanding between business needs, human relationships, and technical acumen. My experience is in transforming the software arm of a business for growth: support of business development through technical and product innovation, identification of technical opportunities to streamline or "productize" project-based and legacy software, and technical execution with the engineering team.

Professionally, I've run teams in size from just a few developers to over 100 employees across multiple disciplines on 3 continents. I've defined, executed, and repeated instilling proper processes and methodologies to scale growth and improve quality. My business experience includes project and program management (with project values in the double-digit millions), P/L for projects and departments within a vertical. I've executed successful R&D programs, designing and executing new products and features as well as significant improvements to existing baselines. Millions of people every day use software I've designed, writtern, managed, executed, or implemented - a fact I'm quite proud of. Dedication to process and exellence has allowed my group to develop software recognized as the most accurate and reliable in the world. This same dedication has allowed us to successfully implement projects across the globe.

"It's not a destination but a journey" is an overused phrase, to be sure, but describes accurately how a business should view its ongoing path for growth. Software and product development that supports growth is more than an afterthought, more than "we need to hire 3 more people to make this list of features". It requires an experienced professional with the business skill, technical acumen, and practical experience to lead.

When I'm not building teams and products, I'm devoted to my rescue greyhounds, host foreign exchange students, love to cook, and engage in personal travel. I enjoy craft cocktails - creating and consuming - but I considered not putting that here, at risk of sounding like a drunkard. I guess I also believe in moderation, in all things. I love to see and experience new things, often putting myself out of my comfort zone. It would be a stretch to refer to myself as even an amateur photographer, but all of the pictures on this site are by me from my travels.
